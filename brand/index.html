07 Voice & Tone

Restrained. Cinematic. Sensory. Implied.

Restrained and observational

The prose watches rather than pushes. Descriptions let events unfold without editorial pressure.

Cinematic and sensory

Scenes are rendered visually, through atmosphere, light, texture, and spatial awareness.

Clear and controlled

Sentences are precise and deliberate, not showy or self-consciously stylish.

Implied worldbuilding

Context does the heavy lifting. Technology and culture emerge through action, never exposition.

Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • Let the premise speak for itself
  • Use concrete, sensory details
  • Trust the reader to infer
  • Keep sentences clear and controlled
  • Imply mystery through what is unsaid
  • Use italics for ship names: Demeter

Don't

  • Oversell with hyperbole
  • Use vague, abstract descriptions
  • Explain everything explicitly
  • Write punchy, fragmented marketing copy
  • Reveal too much in promotional text
  • Use bold or ALL CAPS for emphasis in body

09 Accessibility

WCAG 2.2 as aspirational guideline. Incremental improvement.

Current State

  • Basic alt attributes on images. No ARIA roles. No explicit focus management.
  • Noto Sans 200 on dark backgrounds may not meet AA contrast at all sizes.
  • No prefers-reduced-motion query implemented.

Direction

  • Add ARIA roles incrementally: role="dialog", aria-modal, focus trapping for modals.
  • Ensure visible focus indicators on all interactive elements.
  • Consider Noto Sans 400 for small or critical text where contrast is marginal.
  • Add prefers-reduced-motion media query to respect user motion preferences.

Audience

Primary

Adult science fiction readers who appreciate literary-leaning genre fiction, atmospheric worldbuilding, and character-driven narratives. They respond to craft over spectacle.

Secondary

Bookstore owners and buyers evaluating whether to stock the title. Materials for this audience must be professional, concise, and respect their time.

17 Vocabulary & Language

Word choices, preferred terms, and language to avoid.

Preferred Terms

  • Specific, concrete nouns over abstract ones
  • Sensory verbs: stirs, plunges, unfolds, emerges, awakens
  • Ship names italicized: Demeter
  • "Debut novel" for the first book
  • "Speculative fiction" as the genre umbrella
  • "Emerges" rather than "is revealed"

Terms to Avoid

  • Exclamation-heavy marketing: "Amazing!", "Don't miss!"
  • Over-explanation of sci-fi concepts
  • Cliched genre openings: "In a world where..."
  • Empty superlatives: "utterly unique", "unlike anything"
  • Fragmented, punchy copy -- the voice is controlled, not staccato
  • Bold or ALL CAPS for emphasis in body copy

Ship Name Formatting (CMOS)

Rules

  • Italicize the ship name only. Prefixes (ESS, USS, HMS) remain roman. The possessive 's also remains roman.
    Example: ESS Demeter's crew   (CMOS 17th ed., 8.116)
  • When a ship name appears within already-italicized text, reverse the formatting -- the ship name reverts to roman.
    Example: I hope that Demeter is okay.   (CMOS 17th ed., 7.55)
  • Do not italicize "the" preceding a ship name unless it is part of the official name.
    Correct: the Demeter   Incorrect: the Demeter   (CMOS 17th ed., 8.116)

Formality Level

Scale

  • Neutral to slightly formal. Literary but accessible.
  • No academic jargon. No casual slang.
  • Genre vocabulary (telepathic, interstellar, sentient, rootwork) used naturally without exposition.
  • Do not apologize for or distance from the genre.

Writing Examples

Good

"Once the pride of humanity's interstellar fleet, the ESS Demeter is on a final voyage to Tau Ceti. But something stirs within the ship's rootwork, hidden and ancient, unfolding."

Restrained. Sensory. Lets the reader's imagination fill the gaps.

Avoid

"In an incredible journey through the stars, the amazing crew of the Demeter must fight for their very survival in this thrilling, action-packed, must-read debut!"

Hyperbolic. Empty superlatives. Fragmented marketing tone.
